## Why Rainwater Collection Matters

In the Dominican Republic, access to clean water can be challenging, especially for street dogs. Our integrated rainwater collection system ensures that every shelter provides a reliable source of fresh water.

## Materials Needed

- 1 large plastic bottle (2L or larger)
- Small food-grade container or bowl
- Flexible tubing (recycled from IV bags if possible)
- Zip ties or rope
- Drill or sharp tool for making holes

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare the Collection Bottle

Clean your plastic bottle thoroughly. Drill a small hole near the bottom—this will be where the water flows out.

### Step 2: Install the Tubing

Insert one end of the tubing into the hole you just made. Secure it with zip ties or rope to prevent leaks.

### Step 3: Position the Bowl

Place the food-grade container below the bottle. The other end of the tubing should drip directly into this bowl.

### Step 4: Mount Everything

Attach the bottle upside-down to the shelter roof or side. When it rains, water collects in the bottle and slowly drips into the bowl, keeping it constantly refreshed.

## Pro Tips

- Clean the system monthly to prevent algae growth
- Use dark-colored bottles to reduce algae
- Position the bowl in shade to keep water cool
